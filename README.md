In this document, we present a comprehensive overview of our computational framework designed for drug discovery, leveraging advanced molecular modeling techniques to facilitate efficient and reliable predictions.

## Scientific Background
Understanding the molecular architecture of biological systems is essential for drug discovery. Traditional methods often lack the necessary resolution, leading to an increasing reliance on computational tools that provide insights into molecular interactions.

## Pipeline Architecture
Our framework comprises a modular architecture that integrates various state-of-the-art techniques for structure prediction, ligand preparation, and molecular docking.

## Module-wise Deep Explanations
### Structure Prediction
This module utilizes state-of-the-art algorithms to predict the three-dimensional conformation of target macromolecules.
### Pocket Detection
Identifying binding pockets in proteins is crucial for understanding potential interaction sites for ligands.
### Ligand Preparation
The ligand preparation module ensures that ligands are in the optimal form for docking experiments.
### Docking
Our docking strategies implement advanced algorithms that simulate the interaction between the target and ligands to predict binding affinities.
### ML Rescoring
Machine learning models are employed to reevaluate docking poses and improve the accuracy of binding predictions.
### ADMET
The ADMET module assesses the absorption, distribution, metabolism, excretion, and toxicity profiles of the compounds.
### Visualization
We provide tools for visualizing molecular interactions and simulations to facilitate analyses and interpretations.
